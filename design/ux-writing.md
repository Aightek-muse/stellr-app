# UX Writing Guide — Stellr

**Version:** 1.0  
**Updated:** 2026-04-07

---

## Voice & Tone

### Voice (Always)

**Warm, wise, specific, validating.** Like a knowledgeable friend who takes you seriously.

### Tone (By Context)

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Onboarding | Inviting, low-pressure | "Answer 7 questions about who you are." |
| Questions | Gentle, non-judgmental | "What's your relationship with your emotions?" |
| Reveals | Poetic, specific, resonant | "You feel things at a depth others rarely reach." |
| Daily reading | Grounded, present | "Today's energy favours depth over surface." |
| Errors | Calm, helpful | "Something didn't connect. Check your signal and try again." |
| Paywall | Honest, benefit-led | "Go beyond your Big 3." |

---

## Writing Rules

1. **Second person always:** "You feel..." not "Scorpios feel..."
2. **Present tense:** "You crave..." not "You will crave..."
3. **Validating, not prescriptive:** "This might resonate" not "This IS you"
4. **No jargon without explanation:** Define terms when first used
5. **No guilt, no urgency:** "Unlock" not "Don't miss out"
6. **Specific over generic:** "The part of you most people never see" not "Learn about your emotions"

## Question Flow Copy

### Q1 — Energy Patterns

**Question:**
> When do you feel most like yourself?

**Options:**
> A. In the morning, when everything feels possible  
> B. Late at night, when the world goes quiet  
> C. Around people — their energy lights you up  
> D. Alone, deep in your own thoughts

---

### Q2 — Conflict Response

**Question:**
> When something bothers you, what do you usually do?

**Options:**
> A. Bring it up directly — you'd rather clear the air  
> B. Let it go and hope it fades on its own  
> C. Sit with it until you know what you actually feel  
> D. Find a way for everyone to meet in the middle

---

### Q3 — Emotional Expression

**Question:**
> What's your relationship with your emotions?

**Options:**
> A. You feel deeply and people usually know it  
> B. You feel deeply but keep most of it to yourself  
> C. You tend to stay fairly even — highs and lows don't hit hard  
> D. You process through thinking, not feeling

---

### Q4 — Social Energy

**Question:**
> After a long, draining week, you most want to:

**Options:**
> A. Go out — being around people recharges you  
> B. Spend the evening with one person you trust  
> C. Be completely alone, with no obligations  
> D. Lose yourself in something creative

---

### Q5 — Decision Making

**Question:**
> When you're facing a big decision, you tend to:

**Options:**
> A. Trust your gut — it usually knows before you do  
> B. Make a list and think it through carefully  
> C. Ask people you trust what they think  
> D. Sleep on it and see what feels right in the morning

---

### Q6 — Creative Expression

**Question:**
> Which of these feels most like *you*?

**Options:**
> A. Words — writing, speaking, storytelling  
> B. Visuals — images, design, the way things look  
> C. Sound — music, rhythm, the feeling of a song  
> D. Making — building things, working with your hands

---

### Q7 — Life Focus

**Label:**
> Last one.

**Question:**
> What area of life feels most alive right now?

**Options:**
> A. Work and what you're building in the world  
> B. Relationships — who you love and how you connect  
> C. Understanding yourself more deeply  
> D. Exploration — new places, ideas, experiences

## CTA Framework

| Goal | CTA Formula | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Primary action | Action + [specific benefit] | "Unlock My Full Chart", "Share My Moon Sign" |
| Secondary action | Low-commitment verb | "Maybe later", "Continue with free", "Not now" |
| Onboarding | Discovery framing | "Find My Signs", "Discover your..." |

**Avoid:**
- "Subscribe Now" (transactional)
- "Upgrade" (vague)
- "Purchase" (cold)
